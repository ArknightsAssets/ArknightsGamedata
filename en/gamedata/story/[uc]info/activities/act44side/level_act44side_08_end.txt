By the riverside of Kajimachi, beneath the fireworks, Mifune spills the beans about the twisted hatred he has accumulated for Tessai and the others, but also shows a hint of tiredness and longing before the final showdown. Listening to the current state of Mitsukue—a place no longer recognizable as the hometown she once knew—Hoshiguma burns with the flames of rage, not just for Tessai, and not only at Mifune.
